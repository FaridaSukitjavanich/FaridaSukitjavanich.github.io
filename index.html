Psychology

My name is Farida Sukitjavanich, and I am a Psychology major at the University of California, Riverside. I am working toward my bachelor’s degree with an expected graduation date of June 2028. I chose psychology because I’ve always been fascinated by how people think, feel, and behave, and I hope to build a career that allows me to support others, promote emotional well-being, and make a meaningful difference in people’s lives.

Through my academic journey at UCR, I have developed a strong interest in areas such as cognitive psychology, mental health, and the ways our environments shape who we become. I enjoy connecting what I learn in class to real experiences and relationships in my daily life. Over time, I’ve become even more passionate about understanding people on a deeper level and finding ways to create healthier, more supportive communities.

Outside of school, I have gained valuable professional experience working in the restaurant and hospitality industry. I have worked as both a front-of-house server and an assistant manager, which has helped me build strong communication skills, leadership skills, and the ability to work well under pressure. These roles require patience, teamwork, and adaptability, and they have given me the opportunity to meet many different kinds of people. I’ve learned how to stay calm during busy shifts, resolve conflicts quickly, and support both customers and coworkers with empathy.

My long-term goal is to work in a field that allows me to help others. I am interested in exploring clinical psychology, counseling, or other areas that focus on mental health and emotional support. I want to build a career where I can listen to people’s stories, understand their challenges, and help guide them toward healthier and more fulfilling lives.

In addition to my academic and professional interests, I enjoy finding balance in my personal life. Spending time with friends, discovering new food spots, exploring creative projects, and expressing myself through social media are all things that bring me joy. Creating this website is an exciting step for me because it highlights my growth, accomplishments, and my identity as a psychology student.
